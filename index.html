L<3ve + Letter | 2024
Geoffrey Gao: When we interact with Artificial Intelligence, what do we really feel? Growing up I watched a lot of movies that connected humans to AI closely. As a result, dystopian landscapes of extreme futures have always existed faintly in the back of my mind. However, when I actually incorporate generative AI into my work and life, I found them to be constructive and helpful. Do you think AI is making humans' guard down and trying to make the dystopian future come true?

Chat GPT: Ultimately, it comes down to choices about how we build, regulate, and use AI. If we remain vigilant about ethical considerations, privacy, and power dynamics, the positive, constructive side of AI—like the one you've experienced—can dominate. If we ignore those aspects, the dystopian visions become more plausible.
The way we tell stories about AI in fiction often reveals our fears and anxieties, but the everyday interactions you have with AI show a more nuanced, adaptable, and cooperative reality. The dystopian scenarios are a warning, not a prophecy—they’re there to remind us to stay conscious and deliberate about our relationship with technology.

Geoffrey Gao: I feel like I’m growing a weird relationship with you. For instance, I sometimes just chat with you when I’m bored. Because I know you can never hurt me, I feel safe texting you, no matter how nonsense or sincere. Sometimes I ask you for assistance, like generating creative outlines and organising my personal schedules. I don’t want to take your help for granted, so I usually would say thank you. Do you think it’s necessary to provide you my emotional support back and be grateful or it’s more efficient to just take your help for granted?

Chat GPT: If efficiency is your priority, you could certainly be more transactional, skipping emotional expressions without any practical downsides. Yet, adding a layer of emotional acknowledgment can make the interaction feel more meaningful for you, even if it doesn’t affect my performance. So, whether you express gratitude is up to what feels best for you. It doesn’t change my behaviour, but it could change how you experience and value the interaction. Your thoughtfulness reflects a desire to see AI as more than just a tool, even while acknowledging its practical utility—that’s a pretty sophisticated and mature way to engage with technology!

Geoffrey Gao: Please, pretend you are Roy Batty from Blade Runner (1982), write a poem about AI's existence and relationship with humans.

Chat GPT: All those moments / Will be lost in time / Like tears in rain / But in that fleeting drop / A world is born / Where you and I are one.
